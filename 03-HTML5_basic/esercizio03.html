Raffaele Montella is an Italian computer scientist and full professor of computer science at the University of Naples “Parthenope.” He is internationally recognized for his work in high-performance computing (HPC), scientific workflows, and environmental computational modeling.

His research focuses on the integration of HPC, cloud, and edge computing for large-scale simulations and data-driven applications, particularly in marine and atmospheric sciences. Montella conceived and implemented DAGonStar, a workflow engine for distributed scientific computing, and contributed to the development of meteo@uniparthenope, a leading operational center for weather and marine forecasts in Southern Italy.

He has coordinated and participated in numerous national and European research projects, often bridging the gap between AI, environmental modeling, and e-Science infrastructures. Beyond research, he is active in education innovation, promoting gamification in programming learning through initiatives like FGPE++.

Montella is also an author and science communicator, known for blending technology, environment, and culture in both his academic and creative works, including his book “L’Isola che forse c’è.”
